About BMI

What Does BMI Stand For?

BMI, or Body Mass Index, is a measure used to assess a person's weight relative to their height. It's calculated by dividing an individual's weight in kilograms by the square of their height in meters. This method provides a straightforward way to gauge whether someone is within a healthy weight range.

What Is the Ideal BMI for Men and Women?

The ideal BMI range for adults, regardless of gender, is typically considered to be between 18.5 and 24.9. A BMI below 18.5 indicates underweight, while a BMI of 25.0 or higher suggests overweight. Individuals with a BMI of 30.0 or above are classified as obese.

BMI Categories

Below 18.5: Underweight
18.5-24.9: Normal Weight
25.0-29.9: Overweight
30.0 and above: Obese

Health Risks of Being Overweight

  • Type 2 diabetes
  • Heart conditions
  • Elevated LDL cholesterol levels and reduced HDL cholesterol levels, increasing the likelihood of coronary artery diseases and heart attacks
  • High triglycerides
  • Stroke
  • High blood pressure
  • Gall bladder-related complications
  • Insulin resistance
  • Osteoarthritis or joint cartilage breakdown leading to joint pain
  • Sleep apnea or intermittent breathing obstruction during sleep
  • Atherosclerosis or cholesterol deposits buildup in blood vessels (arteries)
  • Skin health issues like skin tags and darkening of the skin
  • Difficulty breathing
  • Hernia
  • Varicose veins
  • Cataracts
  • Gout
  • Stress incontinence or frequent urge to urinate
  • Increased risk of certain cancers (breast, colon, endometrial, gall bladder, kidney, liver)
  • Clinical depression and other mental health disorders
  • Polycystic ovarian disease in women leading to reduced fertility and menstrual irregularities

Health Risks of Being Underweight

Having a BMI below 18.5 poses various health risks, including:

  • Vitamin deficiencies
  • Anemia (low red blood cell count leading to reduced oxygen-carrying capacity)
  • Decreased immunity, making one more susceptible to infections
  • Osteoporosis or brittle bones prone to fractures
  • Malnutrition
  • Developmental delays in children
  • Hormonal imbalances
  • Menstrual cycle disruptions in women, leading to conception complications

Being underweight may also indicate underlying health conditions like anorexia. Therefore, if your BMI falls within the underweight category, it's advisable to seek medical advice.
